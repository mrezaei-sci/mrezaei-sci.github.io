Research

From brain signals and networks to interpretable AI.

I build research pipelines that connect brain and physiological signals, network-level neural dynamics, and machine learning to questions about cognition, learning, and individual differences — with interpretability at the center.

Research themes

Four connected threads spanning neuroimaging, biosignals, and computational modeling.

1) Brain networks in learning & decision-making

I use task and resting-state fMRI with effective- and directed-connectivity methods (DCM, Granger) to study how brain networks reorganize during motor learning and decision-making, linking neural organization to measurable behavior such as accuracy, speed, and decision quality.

Task-fMRIrs-fMRIEffective connectivityDirected networks

2) EEG & biosignal analysis

I work on EEG/EMG and physiological signal analysis — preprocessing, time–frequency analysis, spectral entropy, and information decomposition — to extract temporally precise markers of attention, emotion, fatigue, and decision-making under pressure.

EEGSpectral entropyInformation decompositionBayesian filtering

3) Interpretable NeuroAI & clinical biomarkers

I build deep learning models that are accountable for their decisions — using Grad-CAM-style explanations, ablation, and calibration. In my Alzheimer's work I integrated voxel-based morphometry, cortical thickness, and other MRI-derived measures into interpretable CNN/FCN classifiers.

VBMCAT12/SPM12CNN/FCNExplainable AI

4) Movement & human-state modeling

I also study human movement and performance from 3D skeleton time series and physiological signals — temporal deep learning with interpretable feedback for rehabilitation and skill-learning contexts. This connects naturally to my interest in adaptive, human-centered systems.

RGB-D skeletonsTCNGrad-CAMRehabilitation AI

Current directions

A plain-language version for busy PIs and collaborators.

Motor learning & brain-network reconfiguration

My M.Sc. thesis studies how motor practice reorganizes cortical and subcortical networks, and how this relates to learning components such as accuracy and speed. I care about connectivity methods that stay interpretable and statistically reliable in high-dimensional data.

M.Sc. thesisMotor learningAccuracy-speed networks

Toward NeuroAI & developmental neuroscience

I want to use NeuroAI — artificial neural networks as computational models of the brain — together with multimodal data (EEG, behavior, genomics) to understand where and how neural processing differs across individuals, especially in development and clinical populations.

NeuroAIMultimodal integrationIndividual differences

Research goal

I want to develop computational and AI methods for neuroscience that are not only accurate, but understandable and useful in real settings. My aim is to connect brain activity, physiological signals, behavior, and machine learning into tools that advance our understanding of cognition, learning, and clinical assessment.
