Current thesis direction

A plain-language version for busy PIs.

My M.Sc. thesis studies how motor practice reorganizes cortico–subcortical loops and how that reconfiguration maps onto distinct learning components (accuracy vs speed/vigor). I’m interested in directed connectivity as a mechanistic lens — and in inference workflows that remain stable under high-dimensional multiple testing.
