Research

From movement and biosignals to brain networks and interpretable AI.

I build research pipelines that connect human movement, brain and physiological signals, and machine learning to clinically and behaviorally meaningful outcomes.

Research themes

My work combines biomedical engineering, AI, neuroimaging, and human performance analysis.

1) Human movement analysis and rehabilitation AI

I work on movement-quality assessment using 3D skeleton time series, RGB-D skeletal data, temporal deep learning, and interpretable feedback. My recent work used KIMORE squat recordings to classify movement quality and identify the movement phases that most influenced the model decision.

RGB-D skeletons Movement assessment TCN Grad-CAM

2) Biosignal processing and human state estimation

I am interested in EEG, EMG, and physiological signal analysis for understanding attention, fatigue, pressure, engagement, and learning. This direction is especially important for human-in-the-loop systems, rehabilitation feedback, and adaptive training technologies.

EEG EMG Spectral entropy Human state

3) Brain networks in learning and decision-making

I use task and resting-state fMRI to study how brain networks support motor learning, decision-making, and performance. My focus is on connectivity patterns that link neural organization to measurable behavior such as accuracy, speed, and decision quality.

Task-fMRI rs-fMRI Brain networks Motor learning

4) Deep morphometry biomarkers

I also work on structural MRI and biomarker-guided deep learning. In my Alzheimer’s disease project, I integrated voxel-based morphometry, cortical thickness, WMH, and other MRI-derived measures with CNN/FCN models to support interpretable disease classification.

VBM CAT12/SPM12 CNN/FCN Alzheimer's disease

Current directions

A plain-language version for busy PIs and collaborators.

Motor learning and brain-network reconfiguration

My M.Sc. thesis studies how motor practice reorganizes cortical and subcortical brain networks, and how this reconfiguration relates to different learning components such as accuracy and speed. I am interested in connectivity methods that remain interpretable and statistically reliable in high-dimensional neuroimaging data.

M.Sc. thesis Motor learning Accuracy-speed networks

AI for personalized rehabilitation and training

A major goal of my future work is to combine movement analysis, biosignals, and machine learning to build systems that assess human performance and provide meaningful feedback. I am especially interested in rehabilitation engineering, adaptive training, human-in-the-loop systems, and interpretable AI.

Rehabilitation AI Human-in-the-loop Adaptive feedback
