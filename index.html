About Me

I’m a biomedical engineer turned neural tinkerer, fascinated by how structure becomes computation in the human brain. My work blends neuroimaging, signal processing, and deep learning to push diagnostics—especially for neuro-degenerative and performance-critical domains—beyond what we can see with the naked eye.

When I’m not reverse-engineering neurons, you’ll find me on the football pitch, analyzing movement patterns (yes, still data-driven!), or tinkering with hardware hacks that turn lab ideas into clinic-ready tools.
