Movement AI · Rehabilitation · Conference paper

Interpretable skeleton-based deep learning for squat-quality assessment

Developed an end-to-end pipeline for automated squat-quality assessment using KIMORE 3D skeleton time-series data. The workflow includes temporal alignment, body-centered normalization, bone-direction features, motion derivatives, CNN-BiLSTM and Temporal Convolutional Network models, and Grad-CAM-style temporal explanations.

The goal was not only to classify a movement as correct or incorrect, but also to show which phase of the movement influenced the model decision. This makes the work relevant to rehabilitation feedback, coaching, and human movement assessment.

EEG · Sports neuroscience · Under review

Frontal EEG spectral entropy and pressure-induced decision failure

Studied how frontal EEG complexity changes during decision-making under pressure in youth soccer players. The project used EEG signal-processing workflows including filtering, spectral analysis, entropy estimation, and group-level statistical testing.

This work supports my broader interest in using biosignals to estimate internal human states such as attention, pressure, fatigue, engagement, and performance failure during training or rehabilitation.

NeuroImage · Task-fMRI · Published

Neural efficiency in creative soccer decision-making

Analyzed task-fMRI data from soccer experts to study creative decision-making during performance-relevant action choices. The project focused on sparse directed brain-network patterns and how expert performance may rely on efficient network organization.

This work was published in NeuroImage and strengthened my experience in neuroimaging, network neuroscience, statistical inference, scientific visualization, and manuscript development.

NeuroImage: Reports · Structural MRI · Published

Early Alzheimer's diagnosis with VBM and deep learning

Integrated structural MRI biomarkers with voxel-based morphometry and deep learning for Alzheimer’s disease and MCI classification. The project used biologically meaningful measures such as gray matter volume, white matter volume, CSF, white matter hyperintensity, and cortical thickness.

These biomarkers were transformed into multi-bit heatmap representations and used with CNN/FCN models to support interpretable disease classification.

Resting-state fMRI · Motor learning · Under review

Cortical-subcortical network reconfiguration after motor learning

My M.Sc. thesis studies how motor practice reorganizes cortical and subcortical brain networks, and how this reconfiguration relates to learning components such as accuracy, speed, and performance improvement.

The project connects resting-state fMRI, connectivity analysis, motor learning, and behavioral outcomes, with a focus on interpretable network-level mechanisms.

EEG · Sleep neuroscience · Under review

Integrated Information Decomposition in REM dream emotion

Applied information-theoretic analysis to REM pre-awakening EEG to study how dream emotional content may be reflected in hemispheric information-flow patterns.

The project uses EEG preprocessing, alpha-band analysis, mixed-effects modeling, surrogate testing, and information decomposition to investigate affective dream content.

EEG signal processing · Bayesian filtering

EEG ocular artifact removal using particle filtering

Designed a Bayesian denoising framework to estimate clean EEG activity while tracking time-varying ocular contamination from EOG-related artifacts.

The project focused on non-Gaussian and non-stationary noise conditions, where conventional linear filtering can distort task-relevant neural rhythms.

EEG Particle filtering Artifact correction Bayesian modeling

Computational neuroscience · Spiking models

Criticality in excitatory-inhibitory neural networks

Simulated excitatory-inhibitory spiking neural networks using Izhikevich neuron models to study synchrony, phase transitions, and avalanche-like activity.

This work helped me connect biological neural dynamics with computational modeling, network stability, and information-processing regimes.

Izhikevich model Spiking networks Criticality Dynamical systems

How these projects connect

Across these projects, my main goal is to build systems that make complex biological and behavioral data more useful. Whether the data come from skeleton motion, EEG/EMG, structural MRI, or fMRI, I am interested in models that are accurate, interpretable, and connected to real human outcomes such as rehabilitation progress, skill learning, decision quality, and clinical assessment.
