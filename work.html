NeuroImage · Task-fMRI · Published

Neural efficiency in creative decision-making

Analyzed task-fMRI from expert athletes to study creative decision-making, focusing on sparse directed brain-network patterns and how expertise may rely on efficient network organization.

Published in NeuroImage; strengthened my experience in network neuroscience, statistical inference, scientific visualization, and manuscript development.

Resting-state fMRI · Motor learning · Under review

Cortical–subcortical network reconfiguration after motor learning

My M.Sc. thesis studies how motor practice reorganizes cortical and subcortical brain networks, and how this reconfiguration relates to accuracy, speed, and performance improvement.

Connects resting-state fMRI, connectivity analysis, and behavioral outcomes, with a focus on interpretable network-level mechanisms.

EEG signal processing · Bayesian filtering

EEG ocular artifact removal using particle filtering

Designed a Bayesian denoising framework to estimate clean EEG activity while tracking time-varying ocular contamination from EOG-related artifacts.

Targeted non-Gaussian, non-stationary noise conditions where conventional linear filtering distorts task-relevant neural rhythms — built around preserving downstream ERP, time–frequency, and classification features.

Particle filteringArtifact correctionBayesian modeling

Applied information-theoretic analysis to REM pre-awakening EEG to study how dream emotional content is reflected in hemispheric information-flow patterns.

Uses EEG preprocessing, alpha-band analysis, mixed-effects modeling, surrogate testing, and integrated information decomposition.

EEG · Cognitive neuroscience · Under review

Frontal EEG spectral entropy & decision failure under pressure

Studied how frontal EEG complexity changes during decision-making under evaluative pressure, using filtering, spectral analysis, entropy estimation, and group-level statistical testing.

Supports my broader interest in using biosignals to estimate internal states such as attention, pressure, fatigue, and performance failure.

Computational neuroscience · Spiking models

Criticality in excitatory–inhibitory neural networks

Simulated excitatory–inhibitory spiking networks using Izhikevich neuron models to study synchrony, phase transitions, and avalanche-like activity.

Combined numerical simulation with mean-field and bifurcation concepts to examine how E/I balance shapes network stability and information-processing regimes.

NeuroImage: Reports · Structural MRI · Published

Early Alzheimer's diagnosis with VBM and deep learning

Integrated structural MRI biomarkers with voxel-based morphometry and deep learning for Alzheimer's and MCI classification, using gray/white matter volume, CSF, WMH, and cortical thickness.

Biomarkers were transformed into multi-bit heatmap representations and used with CNN/FCN models for interpretable classification.

Movement AI · Rehabilitation · Conference paper

Interpretable skeleton-based deep learning for squat-quality assessment

An end-to-end pipeline for automated movement-quality assessment using KIMORE 3D skeleton time series — temporal alignment, body-centered normalization, bone-direction features, motion derivatives, CNN-BiLSTM/TCN models, and Grad-CAM-style temporal explanations.

Goes beyond correct/incorrect classification to show which movement phase drove the decision — relevant to rehabilitation feedback and coaching.

How these projects connect

Across these projects, my goal is to make complex biological and behavioral data more interpretable and useful. Whether the data come from fMRI, EEG/EMG, structural MRI, or skeleton motion, I focus on models that are accurate, explainable, and tied to real outcomes — learning, decision quality, and clinical assessment.
